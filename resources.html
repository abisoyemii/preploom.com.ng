TOEFL iBT Practice Questions

Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing sections

📖 Reading Medium
Passage Excerpt: "The Industrial Revolution marked a major turning point in Earth's ecology and humans' relationship with their environment. The impacts on the world's psyche would be catastrophic if the same were to happen with today's industry and technology."

According to the passage, what is the author's main concern regarding modern industry and technology?

  • A That they will cause more pollution than the Industrial Revolution
  • B That their psychological impact could be devastating if similar changes occur
  • C That they will reverse the ecological progress made since the 1800s
  • D That they represent a less significant change than the Industrial Revolution
✓ Correct Answer: B
The passage explicitly states that "The impacts on the world's psyche would be catastrophic if the same were to happen with today's industry and technology." This directly supports option B. The author is concerned about psychological impacts, not just pollution (A) or ecological reversal (C).
💡 Tip: In TOEFL Reading, look for direct paraphrases of the passage text. The correct answer often uses synonyms for words in the passage (e.g., "catastrophic" = "devastating", "psyche" = "psychological").

📖 Reading – True/False/Not Given Medium
Passage: "Urban vertical farming has emerged as a promising solution to food security challenges. Unlike traditional agriculture, vertical farms use controlled environments, artificial lighting, and hydroponic systems to grow crops in stacked layers inside buildings. These facilities can produce yields up to 100 times greater per square metre than conventional outdoor farms. However, critics point out that the high energy costs of maintaining artificial lighting significantly increase the carbon footprint, potentially offsetting the environmental benefits. Some researchers suggest that pairing vertical farms with renewable energy sources could resolve this issue."

Do the following statements agree with the information in the passage? Write TRUE, FALSE, or NOT GIVEN.

1. Vertical farms use natural sunlight as their primary light source.

2. Vertical farms can produce significantly more food per square metre than traditional farms.

3. The majority of vertical farms currently use solar panels for energy.

✓ Answers: 1=FALSE, 2=TRUE, 3=NOT GIVEN
1. FALSE — The passage states "artificial lighting" is used. 2. TRUE — "up to 100 times greater per square metre" is explicitly stated. 3. NOT GIVEN — Solar panels are mentioned as a possible solution, but it never says most farms currently use them.
💡 Key Rule: NOT GIVEN means the passage neither confirms nor denies. FALSE means the passage directly contradicts the statement. Don't use outside knowledge — base every answer only on the text.

JIMAT (Jaminan Mutu Terpadu) Practice

Reading Comprehension, Grammar, Listening, Writing & Speaking

📖 Reading Comprehension Medium
Passage: "Renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power have become increasingly cost-competitive with fossil fuels over the past decade. Governments worldwide are introducing incentives to accelerate the transition, including tax rebates, feed-in tariffs, and public subsidies for research and development. Despite initial investment costs, renewable installations typically recoup their expenses within 7–10 years and continue generating free energy thereafter. Critics, however, point to grid reliability issues during low-sun or low-wind periods as a significant barrier to complete dependence on renewables."

Which of the following best states the main idea of the passage?

  • ARenewable energy is cheaper than fossil fuels in all circumstances
  • BRenewable energy is increasingly viable but faces practical reliability challenges
  • CGovernments are the only reason renewable energy is growing
  • DFossil fuels remain superior to renewable energy in almost every way
✓ Correct Answer: B
The passage presents both the growing competitiveness and government support for renewables AND acknowledges the criticism about grid reliability. Option B captures this balance. A is too absolute; C ignores cost-competitiveness; D is directly contradicted by the passage.
📝 Grammar – Sentence Completion Easy

Choose the grammatically correct option to complete each sentence.

1. By the time the manager arrived, the team ________ the report.

  • Ahad already completed
  • Bhas completed
  • Ccompleted
  • Dwill complete

2. Neither the director nor the employees ________ informed about the change.

  • Awas
  • Bwere
  • Care
  • Dhas been
✓ Answers: 1=A, 2=B
1. "By the time + past simple" triggers the Past Perfect for the earlier action. 2. With "Neither...nor", the verb agrees with the noun closest to it. "Employees" is plural → "were".
💡 JIMAT Grammar Tip: Watch for subject-verb agreement with correlative conjunctions (either/or, neither/nor, not only/but also). The verb always agrees with the second (closer) subject.

  • B Facilitate a collaborative session to understand both perspectives and reach consensus
  • C Make the architectural decision yourself to keep the project on schedule
  • D Remove both developers from the project and replace them
✓ Correct Answer: B
As a project manager, your role is to facilitate and empower the team. Option B demonstrates servant leadership and conflict resolution skills. 🔢 Technical Medium

Your project has a Budget at Completion (BAC) of $500,000. The Earned Value (EV) is $200,000, Actual Cost (AC) is $250,000, and Planned Value (PV) is $220,000. What is the Cost Performance Index (CPI) and what does it indicate?

  • A CPI = 0.80; the project is over budget
  • B CPI = 1.10; the project is under budget
  • C CPI = 0.91; the project is slightly over budget
  • D CPI = 1.25; the project is significantly under budget
✓ Correct Answer: A (CPI = 0.80)
Formula: CPI = EV / AC
Calculation: $200,000 / $250,000 = 0.80

A CPI less than 1.0 indicates the project is over budget (getting $0.80 of value for every $1.00 spent). The project is also behind schedule (SPI = EV/PV = 0.91).
Related Formulas:
SPI (Schedule Performance Index) = EV / PV = 0.91
CV (Cost Variance) = EV - AC = -$50,000
EAC (Estimate at Completion) = BAC / CPI = $625,000

A user reports their computer is running slowly and the hard drive LED is constantly active. Task Manager shows 100% disk utilization. Which tool should you use FIRST to diagnose the issue?

  • A CHKDSK
  • B System File Checker (SFC)
  • C Resource Monitor (Resmon)
  • D Disk Cleanup
✓ Correct Answer: C (Resource Monitor)

"Living off the Land" (LotL) attacks use legitimate tools (PowerShell, WMI, PsExec) and valid credentials to blend in with normal activity. UEBA establishes baselines of normal behavior and detects anomalies like unusual login times, data access patterns, or tool usage that differ from established profiles.

• Use of built-in Windows tools (PowerShell, WMI)
• Legitimate credentials (often compromised)
• Low-and-slow data exfiltration
• No malware signatures to detect
