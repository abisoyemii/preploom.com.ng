Starting January 21, 2026, the TOEFL iBT underwent one of its biggest transformations in decades. ETS redesigned the test to be shorter, adaptive, and more relevant to real-world academic situations. If you are planning to take the TOEFL in 2026 or later, understanding these updates is essential for your success. For comprehensive TOEFL preparation, visit our TOEFL Prep Course or explore our free study resources.

🚨 Key Takeaway for 2026 Test Takers

The new TOEFL iBT is now under 2 hours total, uses multistage adaptive testing for Reading and Listening, introduces a 1-6 band score scale alongside the traditional 0-120 scale, and features three entirely new Reading task types. Scores are delivered within 72 hours.

What Changed in TOEFL iBT 2026?

ETS officially unveiled the new TOEFL format changes in early 2026, marking a transformative set of updates to the TOEFL iBT exam. These changes aim to modernize the test experience, improve fairness, and better align with the needs of today's global learners.

Major Changes at a Glance

  • Adaptive Testing: Reading and Listening sections now use a multistage adaptive format. The difficulty of questions in the next stage adjusts based on your performance in the previous stage.
  • Reduced Test Time: Total test time is now under 2 hours (previously ~3 hours).
  • New Scoring Scale: A new 1-6 banded score (in half points) is introduced for each section, aligned with the CEFR framework, alongside the traditional 0-120 scale.
  • New Task Types: Three new Reading tasks: "Complete the Words," "Read in Daily Life," and "Read an Academic Passage."
  • Reordered Sections: The new section order is Reading → Listening → Writing → Speaking.
  • Faster Results: Score delivery within 72 hours.
  • Enhanced Audio: Upgraded test center audio with custom-designed stereophones from Koss.

New 1-6 Band Scoring System Explained

Starting January 2026, TOEFL iBT scores are available on a simplified 1-6 band scale in addition to the traditional 0-120 scale. This dual-scale system aligns more closely with the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), making score interpretation easier for both institutions and students.

The total band score is the average of the banded scores from each of the four sections. For example, if you score 4.5 in Reading, 5.0 in Listening, 4.0 in Writing, and 5.5 in Speaking, your total band score would be 4.75 (rounded to 4.5 or 5.0 depending on institutional policies).

Free TOEFL Reading Practice Test 2026: New Task Types & Sample Questions

The Reading section has been completely restructured. Previously featuring two long passages (~700 words each) with 10 questions per passage, the new format introduces a multistage adaptive format with shorter, more diverse texts.

Estimated time: ~27 minutes (reduced from 36 minutes)
Total items: 35-48 (instead of 20 questions)

1. Complete the Words

You will fill in missing letters in academic paragraphs. Each paragraph includes 10 incomplete words. This tests your vocabulary depth and spelling accuracy in an academic context.

2. Read in Daily Life

You will read short, practical texts like emails, announcements, or social media posts (15-150 words each). Each text has 2-3 multiple-choice questions. This reflects real-world campus and daily communication.

Text: Campus Announcement

"The university library will be closed for maintenance from June 15-17, 2026. During this period, all digital resources will remain accessible via the student portal. Physical book returns will be extended automatically. For urgent research needs, contact librarian@university.edu."

Question: What should a student do if they need a physical book during the closure?

✅ Correct Answer: B

3. Read an Academic Passage

You will read a short academic text (around 200 words) with 5 multiple-choice questions. These passages are focused and test your ability to extract key information efficiently.

TOEFL Listening Practice 2026: Adaptive Format & Strategies

The Listening section also adopts the multistage adaptive format. The difficulty adjusts based on your performance, ensuring a personalized and accurate measurement of your listening ability.

Content has been updated to include a wider mix of academic topics and more relevant, modern situations including real-life campus and daily scenarios. The section remains designed to test your ability to understand spoken English in academic contexts.

💡 Listening Strategy for Adaptive Tests

Because the test adapts to your performance, early questions carry significant weight in determining the difficulty level of subsequent questions. Focus intensely on the first few items in each stage to maximize your chances of receiving higher-difficulty (and higher-scoring) questions later.

TOEFL Writing Practice 2026: Academic Discussion Task Sample

The Writing section retains the Academic Discussion Task from the recent previous format. You will read a brief online discussion between a professor and students, then write your own response contributing to the discussion.

You have approximately 29 minutes total for the Writing section. The task requires you to:

  • State and develop your own opinion on the academic topic
  • Contribute meaningfully to the ongoing discussion
  • Use appropriate academic vocabulary and grammar
  • Write at least 100 words (recommended: 120-150 words)

📝 Practice: Academic Discussion Task

Professor: "Many universities are now using AI tools to grade student essays. Some educators believe this improves consistency and frees up time for personalized feedback. Others worry that AI cannot truly assess creativity and critical thinking. What is your position on using AI for essay grading?"

Student A (Alex): "I think AI grading is helpful because it provides instant feedback, which helps us improve faster."

Student B (Jordan): "I disagree. Essays should be evaluated by humans who can understand nuance and original ideas that AI might miss."

Your Task: Write a response of at least 100 words expressing your view and contributing to this discussion.

TOEFL Speaking Practice Test 2026: Updated Task Structure

The Speaking section now appears last in the test sequence (after Writing). This change allows test-takers to build confidence through the earlier sections before tackling the speaking component.

The Speaking section continues to assess your ability to speak English clearly and coherently on academic topics. You will complete tasks that include:

  • Independent speaking: Expressing an opinion on a familiar topic
  • Integrated speaking: Responding to a reading and listening passage
  • Campus situation: Responding to a conversation about a university issue
  • Academic content: Summarizing information from a lecture

Expert Preparation Strategies for TOEFL iBT 2026

1. Adapt to the Shorter Format

With the test now under 2 hours, stamina management is different. Practice with timed sessions of 90-110 minutes to build concentration for the condensed format. The reduced time means there is less room for error — every question counts more.

2. Master the New Reading Tasks

The three new Reading task types require different skills:

  • Complete the Words: Build academic vocabulary through flashcards and spelling drills. Focus on commonly misspelled academic terms.
  • Read in Daily Life: Practice reading real-world texts like university emails, course announcements, and campus bulletins. Focus on extracting key details quickly.
  • Read an Academic Passage: Read short academic abstracts from journals. Practice identifying main ideas, supporting details, and author purpose in 200-word segments.

3. Understand Adaptive Testing Psychology

In adaptive sections, your performance on early questions determines the difficulty of later questions. Do not rush through the first 5-6 questions — accuracy here unlocks higher-value questions later. If you encounter very difficult questions, it means you are performing well.

4. Prepare for Both Scoring Systems

Institutions may reference either the 0-120 scale or the 1-6 band scale. Know your target scores in both systems. For example, a band 5.0 roughly corresponds to 72-94 on the traditional scale.

5. Use Official ETS Resources

ETS launched new preparation resources in early 2026. These include updated practice tests that mirror the adaptive format and new task types. Always prioritize official materials over third-party content for the most accurate preparation.

Important: TOEFL iBT Australia vs Global Version

⚠️ Critical for Australian Visa Applicants

As of January 21, 2026, ETS changed the Global TOEFL iBT to the new 1-6 band score system. However, the Australian Department of Home Affairs does NOT accept this new band score system for visa purposes.

ETS maintains a separate "TOEFL iBT Australia" test that continues to use the traditional 0-120 scoring scale. If you are applying for an Australian visa, you must book the Australia-specific version. Booking the global version will result in invalid scores for Australian immigration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the TOEFL iBT harder in 2026?

The test is not necessarily harder, but it is different. The adaptive format means the test adjusts to your level, providing a more accurate assessment. The shorter duration and new task types require different preparation strategies, but the core English skills being tested remain the same.

Can I still use old TOEFL prep books?

Old prep books are useful for building foundational skills (vocabulary, grammar, listening comprehension), but they will not include the new task types or adaptive format. You should supplement them with official ETS 2026 materials that feature the new question types.

How do I interpret my band score?

Your band score (1-6) corresponds to CEFR levels. A score of 5.0-6.0 is generally required for university admission. Check with your target institution to see which scoring system they prefer.

When will MyBest scores update to the new format?

MyBest scores will continue to be reported on score reports. ETS will combine the best section scores from tests taken within the last two years, regardless of whether they were taken under the old or new format.

Are at-home TOEFL tests still available?

Yes, the TOEFL iBT Home Edition continues to be available. However, verify that your target institution accepts home edition scores, as some programs require in-center testing.
