❓ Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about choosing and taking English proficiency exams

Which English test is the easiest? +

There's no objectively "easiest" test—it depends on your strengths:

  • TOEFL may be easier if you're comfortable with American English, multiple-choice questions, and computer-based testing.
  • IELTS might suit you if you prefer face-to-face interaction and varied question types.
  • Duolingo is shorter and more convenient, but the adaptive format can be challenging.
  • Cambridge tests are comprehensive but allow more time per question.

Take our free diagnostic tests for each exam to see which format feels most natural to you.

Can I use Duolingo instead of TOEFL for US universities? +

It depends on the university. As of 2026:

  • Yes: Over 4,000 universities accept Duolingo, including many state schools and some prestigious institutions.
  • No: Top-tier universities (Ivy League, Stanford, MIT) typically still require TOEFL or IELTS.
  • Conditional: Some universities accept Duolingo for conditional admission, requiring you to take an additional test upon arrival.

Always check your specific target programs' requirements. Our university database above shows which tests each school accepts.

How long should I prepare for these exams? +

Recommended preparation time based on current level and target score:

  • Beginner to Intermediate (B1 → B2): 12-16 weeks
  • Intermediate to Upper-Intermediate (B2 → C1): 8-12 weeks
  • Upper-Intermediate to Advanced (C1 → C2): 4-8 weeks

Factors that affect preparation time:

  • Daily study hours (2-3 hours recommended)
  • Familiarity with test format
  • Specific weak areas (writing often needs more time)
  • Target score gap from current level
What is TOEFL MyBest score and how does it work? +

MyBest scores (also called superscoring) automatically combine your highest section scores from all valid TOEFL tests taken in the last 2 years.

Example:

  • Test 1: Reading 25, Listening 24, Speaking 22, Writing 23 (Total: 94)
  • Test 2: Reading 26, Listening 25, Speaking 24, Writing 22 (Total: 97)
  • MyBest: Reading 26, Listening 25, Speaking 24, Writing 23 (Total: 98)

Important notes:

  • Not all universities accept MyBest scores—check your target schools
  • You must send all test scores used in the MyBest calculation
  • Only available for tests taken after August 1, 2019
Is IELTS Academic or General Training right for me? +

Choose IELTS Academic if:

  • Applying to undergraduate or postgraduate programs
  • Seeking professional registration (medical, nursing, accounting)
  • Need to demonstrate academic English proficiency

Choose IELTS General Training if:

  • Applying for immigration (Express Entry, UK visa, Australia PR)
  • Seeking work experience or training programs
  • Need everyday English proficiency proof

Key differences: Academic has complex texts and graph/chart writing; General has everyday texts and letter writing. Listening and Speaking are identical.

Can I retake the test if I don't get my target score? +

Retake policies by exam:

  • TOEFL: Every 3 days, unlimited attempts. MyBest scores combine best sections.
  • IELTS: No mandatory waiting period, but recommended to prepare adequately between attempts.
  • Duolingo: Maximum 3 tests in any 30-day period. Must wait for results before retaking.
  • Cambridge: No official limit, but results take 2-4 weeks and preparation is intensive.

Cost considerations: Retaking adds up quickly. Consider whether additional preparation might be more cost-effective than multiple attempts.

How do I send scores to universities? +

TOEFL:

  • Free: Select up to 4 institutions before test day
  • Additional: $20 per report, ordered through ETS account
  • Delivery: Electronic (fastest) or mail

IELTS:

  • Free: 5 TRFs (Test Report Forms) to institutions within 1 month of results
  • Additional: ~$20 per institution
  • Delivery: Electronic or courier

Duolingo:

  • Unlimited free sending to any institution
  • Instant electronic delivery
Do these tests expire? How long are scores valid? +

Score validity:

  • TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo: 2 years from test date. After this, institutions typically won't accept scores as they may not reflect current ability.
  • Cambridge: Valid for life. This is a major advantage for career-long certification.

Exceptions: Some immigration programs may require tests taken within 1-2 years of application, even for Cambridge. Always check specific program requirements.

Recommendation: If you need English certification for your career long-term, Cambridge may be worth the additional preparation effort.
