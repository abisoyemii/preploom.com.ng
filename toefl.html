About the TOEFL iBT Exam

The TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test) is the most widely accepted English-language test in the world, recognized by more than 11,500 institutions in over 160 countries. Whether you're planning to study abroad, immigrate, or advance your career, a high TOEFL score opens doors to global opportunities.

Recommended: Get the Official TOEFL iBT Tests Volume 1 & 2 for authentic practice (affiliate link).

Exam Structure 2026

👂 Listening

Duration: 41-57 minutes
Questions: 28-39
Listen to lectures, classroom discussions, and conversations

📖 Reading

Duration: 54-72 minutes
Questions: 30-40
Read 3-4 passages from academic texts

✍️ Writing

Duration: 50 minutes
Tasks: 2 essays
Integrated task + Independent opinion essay

🗣️ Speaking

Duration: 17 minutes
Tasks: 4 responses
Express opinions on familiar topics; discuss reading/listening

Scoring Information

  • Total Score: 0-120 points (sum of 4 sections)
  • Section Scores: 0-30 points each
  • MyBest Scores: Combines best section scores from all tests in last 2 years
  • Score Validity: 2 years from test date
  • Results Available: Approximately 6 days after test

Who Should Take TOEFL?

  • Students planning to study at universities abroad
  • Professionals seeking international certification or licensure
  • Immigration applicants (accepted by many countries)
  • Anyone needing to demonstrate English proficiency for academic or professional purposes

Expert Tips to Score 110+ on TOEFL iBT

1

Master Note-Taking for Listening

Develop a shorthand system using symbols and abbreviations. Focus on key concepts, transitions (however, therefore, in contrast), and speaker attitudes. Practice with TED-Ed lectures and university open courseware. Remember: you won't be graded on your notes, only your answers.

2

Reading: Skim Strategically

Spend 2-3 minutes skimming the entire passage before looking at questions. Identify the main idea, structure, and author's purpose. Don't get stuck on unfamiliar vocabulary—use context clues. Most questions follow the passage order, so use that to locate answers quickly.

3

Speaking: Use Templates

Prepare transition phrases for each task type. For Task 1: "In my opinion...", Task 2: "The man/woman believes...", Task 3: "According to the reading...", Task 4: "The professor discusses...". Practice timing strictly—15 seconds preparation, 45-60 seconds response. Record yourself daily and analyze.

4

Writing: Plan Before You Type

Spend 2-3 minutes outlining your integrated and independent essays. For Integrated: Follow the structure—reading point, listening contrast, repeat. For Independent: Use the "Why + Example" formula. Aim for 300+ words with specific examples. Save 2 minutes for proofreading.

Sample TOEFL iBT Questions

Try these authentic practice questions from our database of 500+ exam-style problems.

Reading Medium

According to the passage, what is the primary function of the mitochondria in eukaryotic cells?

  • A) To store genetic information for protein synthesis
  • B) To convert nutrients into energy through cellular respiration
  • C) To regulate the cell cycle and division processes
  • D) To transport molecules across the cell membrane
Correct Answer: B
Mitochondria are known as the "powerhouses" of the cell because they generate ATP through oxidative phosphorylation, converting nutrients into usable energy. This is explicitly stated in paragraph 3 of the reading passage.
Listening Detail Medium

Listen to the lecture excerpt. What specific example does the professor use to illustrate the concept of cultural diffusion?

  • A) The spread of Buddhism from India
  • B) The adoption of chopsticks in Japan from China
  • C) The development of tea ceremonies in England
  • D) The introduction of rice cultivation in Korea
Correct Answer: B
The professor specifically mentions how chopsticks originated in China but became essential in Japanese cuisine, illustrating how cultural practices can spread and adapt.
Speaking Task 3 Hard

Reading: Professor discusses the "broken windows theory" in criminology.

Lecture: The professor challenges this theory with data from urban renewal projects.

Prompt: Summarize the points made in the reading passage, and then explain how the professor challenges those points.

Response Template (60 seconds):

"The reading passage presents the broken windows theory, which argues that [main point from reading].

However, the professor challenges this view by [specific counterargument from lecture]. For example, [professor's example].

Therefore, the professor believes that [professor's conclusion], contradicting the reading."
Writing Integrated Hard

Reading Passage Summary: The university plans to replace the campus shuttle system with a bike-sharing program.

Lecture Summary: Student explains why this is a bad idea due to [3 specific reasons from lecture].

Prompt: Summarize the proposal in the reading passage and explain why the speaker disagrees with it. Write ~150 words.

Structure Template:

  1. Paragraph 1: Summarize university proposal (2-3 sentences)
  2. Paragraph 2: Student Reason 1 + explanation
  3. Paragraph 3: Student Reason 2 + explanation
  4. Paragraph 4: Student Reason 3 + explanation

Word count: 150-225 words | Time: 20 minutes

Listening Conversation Medium

Listen to the conversation between student and professor. What is the main purpose of the student's visit?

  • A) To request an extension on an assignment
  • B) To complain about the grading policy
  • C) To discuss research opportunities
  • D) To withdraw from the course
Correct Answer: A
The student specifically asks about the possibility of submitting a late assignment due to a family emergency, which was the main point of the conversation.
Speaking Task 2 Hard

Campus Announcement: The university library will extend its hours until midnight during final exam week.

Student Response: Do you agree or disagree with this policy? Use specific reasons and examples to support your answer.

Preparation Strategy (15 seconds):

  • Choose your position immediately (Agree/Disagree)
  • Think of 2 specific reasons with personal examples
  • Prepare opening: "I strongly agree/disagree with..."

Response Template (45 seconds):

"I strongly [agree/disagree] with the library's decision to extend hours.

First of all, [reason 1 with specific example].

Furthermore, [reason 2 with specific example].

For these reasons, I believe this policy is [beneficial/problematic]."

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the TOEFL iBT score valid? +
TOEFL iBT scores are valid for 2 years from the test date. This is standard across all ETS tests. After 2 years, your scores will no longer be accessible in the ETS system, though you can request a score report reprint for tests taken within the last 2 years. Most universities require scores that are valid at the time of application, not necessarily at the time of enrollment.
Can I retake the TOEFL iBT if I'm not satisfied with my score? +
Yes, you can retake the test as many times as you wish, but no more than once in a 3-day period. There is no limit to the number of times you can take the test. Your MyBest scores will automatically combine your highest section scores from all valid tests in the last 2 years. The fee for retaking the test is the same as the original registration fee ($205-$255 depending on location).
What is the difference between TOEFL iBT and IELTS? +
While both test English proficiency, there are key differences: TOEFL iBT is fully computer-based with American English focus, while IELTS offers paper and computer options with British/Australian English. TOEFL speaking is recorded; IELTS speaking is face-to-face with an examiner. TOEFL is preferred by US institutions; IELTS is more widely accepted in UK, Australia, and Canada. TOEFL uses multiple choice extensively; IELTS includes fill-in-the-blank and matching tasks.
How soon can I get my TOEFL results? +
Official scores are available approximately 6 days after your test date (3-4 days for tests taken after April 1, 2026). You will receive an email notification when your scores are ready. PDF score reports can be downloaded from your ETS account, and official reports can be sent to institutions electronically or by mail. Rush reporting is available for an additional fee if you need scores sent within 2-3 days.
Is the TOEFL iBT Home Edition accepted by all universities? +
The Home Edition is accepted by 99% of institutions that accept the TOEFL iBT test. However, it's always recommended to check with your specific target institutions before booking. Some programs, particularly those requiring on-campus presence, may have specific requirements. The Home Edition has the same content, format, and scoring as the test center version.
